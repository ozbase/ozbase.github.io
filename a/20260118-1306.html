Meta may ramp Ray-Ban smart glasses to 20–30M units/year by end of 2026

Source: https://www.gizmodo.jp/2026/01/meta_boosts_smart_glass_production.html

AI Analysis

Meta discussing a ramp to 20–30 million Ray-Ban smart glasses per year (by end of 2026) is a signal that “smart glasses” may be shifting from niche gadget to mass-market accessory. The interesting part is not just the number, but the supply behavior: delaying launches in some countries and prioritizing U.S. inventory suggests demand is real and currently supply-constrained, not just marketing hype. The product lesson is simple: it wins because it looks like normal Ray-Bans first, and a camera/AI device second. Wearables that replace a familiar object tend to scale faster than ones that force a new habit. If this ramp succeeds, the next battleground won’t be hardware specs. It will be privacy norms, app ecosystem, and “what people actually do with a camera on their face” in daily life. Volume makes the category legitimate, and legitimacy attracts competitors. ```
